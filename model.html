Our Business Model

How we turn broken kettles into profit while helping the community

How It Works

Our refurbishment process in 5 simple steps.

1

Collect Broken Kettles

We buy old and broken kettles from households, second-hand shops, and through our trade-in programme across Maseru.

2

Inspect & Assess

Each kettle is taken apart and inspected. We check the heating element, thermostat, seals, plug and cord, then assign a condition grade.

3

Repair & Clean

We replace broken parts, descale the inside, clean the outside and make sure everything works and looks good.

4

Test for Safety

Every kettle is boil tested and all safety features like auto shut-off and boil-dry protection are verified before we sell.

5

Sell with Warranty

We sell through WhatsApp, Facebook and walk-ins at our workshop. Every kettle comes with a 3-month warranty and receipt.

How We Make Money

We have three revenue streams that keep the business growing.

Direct Sales

Our main income. We buy broken kettles cheaply, fix them, and sell at 55-65% profit margin. This is the core of our business.

Repair Services

Customers bring broken kettles for repair. Jobs start from M 50. This gives us steady weekly income and builds loyalty.

Bulk Office Orders

Offices and organisations buy 5+ kettles at a time. These are our biggest single transactions with good margins.
